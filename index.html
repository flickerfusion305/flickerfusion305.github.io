Benchmark and Method for Domain Generalizing Multi-agent Reinforcement Learning

Anonymous Authors
Code

Abstract

Abstract environments in multi-agent reinforcement learning over-simplify real-world settings. To close the gap between laboratory research and real-world deployment we present 6 unique environments packaged in MPEv2, a successor to the highly popular Multi Particle Environments (MPE)—enabling benchmarking of (i) dynamic entity (e.g. agents, obstacles) composition, (ii) intra-trajectory (i.e., within an episode) entity additions, and (iii) out-of-domain generalization. Empirical studies reveal significant weaknesses in existing methods under these real-world inspired circumstances. In response, we propose FlickerFusion, achieving state-of-the-art domain generalization performance vis-à-vis (1) inference reward, and (2) uncertainty reduction. Relative to 11 baselines FlickerFusion ranks first in 10/12 benchmarks, and commonly second. Moreover, FlickerFusion dramatically reduces uncertainty (standard deviation across seeds)—a desireable feature in out-of-domain inference.
